F) Proof of Value (POV) Support

CP4I POV packaging

A POV should be treated as a controlled delivery engagement: clear success criteria, scoped flows, testable acceptance evidence, and a handover pack that removes ambiguity for procurement and operations.

POV scope options

  • Starter (1–2 flows): validate core integration and governance assumptions; prioritise speed and clarity.
  • Standard (2–4 flows): add operational readiness, policy design, and an evidence pack suitable for enterprise decisioning.
  • Enterprise (4–6 flows): include multi-environment concerns, security hardening, performance considerations, and handover-ready SOPs.

Timelines & assumptions

  • Typical timeline: 2–4 weeks (Starter), 3–6 weeks (Standard), 6–10 weeks (Enterprise).
  • Assumptions: timely access provisioning, nominated client stakeholders, and agreed success criteria within week 1.
  • Constraints handled: network restrictions, identity integration, data availability, and environment readiness.
1
Discover

Define objectives, success criteria, stakeholders, and constraints. Confirm target flows and evidence requirements.

2
Design

Solution blueprint, integration patterns, API policies, security controls, and operational model alignment.

3
Build

Implement scoped flows, configurations, and supporting artefacts. Instrument for observability and validation.

4
Validate

Run acceptance scenarios, capture evidence, confirm non-functional concerns in-scope (security, reliability).

5
Handover

Deliver runbooks, SOPs, architecture pack, and recommendations. Enable teams for deployment readiness.

Deliverables (evidence-based)

  • POV charter: objectives, scope, success criteria, acceptance tests, and constraints.
  • Architecture pack: target flow designs, integration patterns, and operational considerations.
  • Demo flows + validation scripts: reproducible scenarios and pass/fail evidence.
  • Security posture summary: identity, secrets handling, policy coverage, audit considerations.
  • Runbooks & SOPs: operations handover for support teams (triage, restart, rollback, monitoring).
  • Executive evidence pack: results, recommendations, and deployment readiness checklist.

Prerequisites checklist (from client)

  • Environment access: required accounts, roles, and approval pathways (least privilege preferred).
  • Connectivity details: network routes, proxies, firewall rules, DNS, and TLS requirements.
  • Data samples: representative payloads, schemas, edge cases, and sanitised test data.
  • System endpoints: source/target systems, auth methods, rate limits, and expected SLAs.
  • Stakeholder availability: product owner, SME(s), security reviewer, and operations representative.
  • Success criteria agreement: what “pass” looks like, how it is evidenced, and who signs off.
